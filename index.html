Connecting elite generations of dynamic leaders, engineers, scientists, and scholars back to India's third premier school campus.

Welcome to Cotton Collegiate Alumni

Since its establishment in 1835, Cotton Collegiate School has been nurturing generations of academic excellence. Today, our alumni are spread across the globe, contributing significantly to society in various fields.

About Our Institution

In Assam, secondary education was launched in the year 1834. Capt. Francis Jenkins, the commissioner of Assam (1834-'61), was the first British official to coordinate structural active measures to provide dynamic instruction for the Assamese youth. Approvals came through the East India Company administration in 1835, creating the foundation of modern educational systems within the province with an enrollment base of 58 students under Headmaster Mr. Singer.

Through foundational structural transformations across the late 19th and early 20th centuries, the institution was raised to a Collegiate tier in 1865, eventually cementing its historic reputation as the Cotton Collegiate School. Today, the modern institution stands bridging three centuries of rich academic inheritance located perfectly within the core heart of Guwahati city.

Vision & Mission

Our Vision

To act as a catalyst to forge a close link between its members and their representative establishments involved with the Institute who seek to share the mission of the Institute to achieve the objective of spreading knowledge to the general good of the country and the society at large.

Our Mission

The Association aims to bring its members spread across the globe under one umbrella, to share the vast treasure of knowledge available in various fields among them and to work in tandem with its' Alma Mater to foster and permeate its ideals across, to the benefits of the society and join hands together for its growth.
